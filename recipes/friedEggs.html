Fried egg

Description

Perfect fried egg is an icon in the food world, but it’s surprisingly tricky to make one at home that looks as good as the diner ideal. The good news? Anyone can master this breakfast staple once they’ve learned a few tips—and I'm here to show you how.

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Butter
  • Salt
  • Freshly Ground Pepper

Steps

Here’s a brief overview of what you can expect when you fry eggs at home:

  1. Crack egg into a small bowl.
  2. In a small nonstick pan over medium heat, melt 1 tsp. butter. Gently swirl to evenly coat pan. If using a metal skillet, heat over medium-high heat until a drop of water sizzles when it hits the pan, then add 1 tsp. oil.
  3. Slip egg into center of pan; season with salt. Cook, undisturbed, until egg white is set on top but yolk is still runny, about 3 minutes. (If desired, use the tines of a fork to break up top of egg white and encourage it to flatten into an even layer.) If the very top of egg white is not set after 3 minutes, add 1 tsp. butter to pan, let melt, then carefully spoon hot melted butter over top of egg white to help it finish cooking.
  4. Season egg with pepper. Using a flexible rubber spatula, transfer egg to a plate.
